Kogi's Lunchbox adventures

The week assignment was to develop a story and characters for an animation. The story had to contain a protagonist, antagonist, helper and an obstacle. We were supposed to have a narrative curve and use the dramaturgical elements. We also had to create a small spritesheet animation.

Idea

I thought it would be nice to create something where children can get familiar with foods from different countries. The idea was that the user can choose from several lunchboxes. Each lunchbox represents the most common food that is eaten for lunch in schools. The main character named Kogi gets put into one of the lunchboxes either by spoon/fork, chopsticks or hand (depending on how people eat in the country the food is from). In each lunchbox Kogi experiences another adventure. In this story Kogi is in a japanese lunchbox. Kogi needs to help the little tomato to cross the river because the little tomato get chased by scary monsters.

Process

My given style was flatdesign. I made a style tile to get to know more about the characteristics of the design. I also worked on the story idea, message and genre. The next step was to create a storyboard and the characters. I thought about how the characters would look like from different sides and how animation principles could be applied to their movements.

Solution

I used HTML and CSS. The sprite sheet was made in Illustrator.

Thoughts

It was interesting to see what goes into developing a small animation and I learned the importance of animation principles for smooth motions.
