Volt landing page + purchase flow

For this project we collaborated with an external client. Volt is a young company that wants to optimise cultural experiences. They provide festival goers with power banks at Roskilde Festival. Almost 15.000 sign up for their service online through the Volt website. Our given task was to improve their Roskilde landing page and the following purchase flow. (How can Volt better explain its services to new customers, simplify the customer journey from the landing page and reduce the bouncing rate?) We had to follow a few design guidelines in regards of the font and colors.

Process

We were a group of 2. Our work process consisted of the design sprint (Map, sketch, Decide, Prototype, test, code).

Research and mapping: We mapped out the problem by analysing Voltâ€™s current solution and their Google Analytics data. We created a user journey and long term goals. We also defined the target group and created a persona based of of that. We made an Eco model to use the insights for the following steps.

Sketching: We sketched ideas and wanted use our insights to adapt and improve the ux.

Deciding: We decided upon a final sketch. We then created a moodboard and a storyboard for the video we wanted to include to attract the target group.

Prototyping: We created a XD prototype for the landing page and a coded version for the purchase flow.

Testing: On day 5 we tested our solution approach. We did the BERT test to see if the webpage translates the feeling we intended to create. We had 11 participants and the result were positive overall. For the purchase flow we did a think aloud test to see if the users think there are any misconceptions or why certain parts are easy to use.

Coding:We splitted up for coding. We coded section by section with the prototype in mind.

You can find the results from our design sprint here.

Solution

I worked on the video mockup, design + sketching and coding the landing page. We used the images and logos provided by Volt, the icons were made in Illustrator. The video mockup was made in After Effects. We used HTML, CSS and Javascript.

In conclusion

My group partner worked on the business parts of the project (for instance the ECO model and Google Analytics data). She explained all her results to me and I was able to learn a lot from her. It was also great to apply all the things we learned in different areas in one project and see how far we can come within only a few days.
